# TweetOS — Grok Packet Templates v3.0
## Refined, Self-Learning, Mobile-Optimized

> These are the three templates your system generates. Each one gets richer over time as your library, engagement log, and learning notes fill up. The more you use TweetOS, the smarter these packets get.

---

## HOW SELF-LEARNING WORKS

The packet generator pulls from four data sources that grow over time:

**1. Performance Library** — every tweet you mark as posted + performance note feeds into the "what works" signal. After 10+ posts, Grok sees patterns not just examples.

**2. Flop Log** — drafts you archived or scored under 50 that you never posted. Grok learns what to avoid.

**3. Engagement Memory** — who replied to you, which reply types got responses, which accounts warmed up. Prevents repetition and prioritizes what's working.

**4. Your own Learning Notes** — at the end of every Grok session, you add one line to a running "what I learned" note. Gets injected next session. This is the highest-signal input — you observed the real world, Grok didn't.

**The packet gets smarter every week. Week 1 it knows you. Week 4 it knows your audience.**

## THE SELF-LEARNING FEEDBACK LOOP

Here's exactly how the packets get smarter over time. Your system auto-populates these from library data. You manually add the learning notes.

### What Auto-Populates (from your TweetOS data)

```
Week 1-2: Profile + examples + basic structure
Week 3-4: + top performing tweets + flop examples
Week 5-6: + topic resonance data + engagement patterns
Week 7+:  + trend performance history + reply type data
          = Grok now knows your audience better than most tools
```

### What You Add Manually (30 seconds after each session)

After every Grok session, before closing the chat, add one line to your running learning notes in TweetOS. That's it. One line.

Examples:
```
"hooks with specific numbers get 2x replies in my niche"
"AI tool frustration + the fix outperforms AI tool frustration alone"
"@buildwithsid replies to student questions every morning — engage then"
"trending model releases: wait 4hrs for drama to settle, then post"
"threads on Saturdays get 3x the engagement of weekday threads"
```

This running log gets injected into all three packets. After 4 weeks it's your most valuable input — because it's observations from the real world that no AI has access to.

### The Growth Signal Stack (in order of power)

```
1. Your running learning notes (highest signal — you observed reality)
2. Accounts that have replied back (warm > cold always)
3. Top performing tweets with performance notes
4. Flop log (knowing what to avoid is underrated)
5. Topic resonance by pillar (which pillars to lean into)
6. Trend performance history (which trends to jump on vs skip)
```

## WHAT GROK WILL AND WON'T GIVE YOU

**Will give you (reliably):**
- Real tweets from last 24-48 hours with links ✅
- Trending topics with context ✅
- Reply and quote tweet options ✅
- Posting time recommendations ✅
- New accounts to engage with ✅
- Hot takes and trend analysis ✅

**Will give you (inconsistently):**
- Exact engagement numbers (likes/retweets) — it can see tweets but count accuracy varies
- Very niche Indian accounts — broad searches work, hyper-specific may miss some

**Will not give you:**
- Your own analytics (use X analytics for that)
- Guaranteed virality (no one can give you this)
- Substitute for actually posting and engaging consistently
